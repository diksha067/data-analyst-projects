Data Science Salaries Dashboard – Explanation
1. Project Overview

The Data Science Salaries Dashboard provides insights into salary trends in the data science industry. It analyzes different factors such as experience level, job roles, company size, remote work ratio, and employee residence.

The dashboard helps organizations and job seekers understand salary distribution, workforce structure, and remote work trends in the data science field.

2. Average Salary by Experience Level

This bar chart shows the average salary in USD for different experience levels including Entry Level (EN), Mid Level (MI), Senior Level (SE), and Executive Level (EX).

Insight:
The chart indicates that Executive level professionals earn the highest salaries, followed by Senior level employees, while Entry level professionals receive the lowest average salary.

3. Work Year Distribution by Experience Level

The pie chart represents the distribution of work records by experience level.

Insight:
The largest portion of employees belongs to the Senior level category, followed by Mid level, while Executive level positions represent the smallest share.

4. Remote Work Ratio by Company Size

The donut chart shows the distribution of remote work based on company size (Small, Medium, and Large companies).

Insight:
Medium-sized companies contribute the most to remote work opportunities, while small companies have comparatively fewer remote roles.

5. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The dashboard contains three KPI cards showing important summary metrics.

Total Salary: 197M
Represents the total salary amount of all employees in the dataset.

Average Remote Ratio: 70.92
Indicates that a large percentage of data science jobs allow remote work.

Count of Job Titles: 607
Represents the total number of job roles included in the dataset.

Insight:
These KPIs provide a quick overview of the overall salary distribution and job diversity in the data science industry.

7. Dashboard Filters

The dashboard includes interactive filters such as:

Job Title Filter
Allows users to analyze salary trends for specific job roles.

Employee Residence Filter
Allows users to view salary data based on employee location.

These filters help users customize the analysis according to their needs.

Conclusion:
The Data Science Salaries Dashboard provides valuable insights into salary patterns, experience distribution, and remote work trends in the data science industry. It helps companies, analysts, and job seekers understand how salaries vary based on experience, job roles, and company size, enabling better career and business decisions.
